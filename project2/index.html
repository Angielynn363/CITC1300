Anterior Lens Retina

The Front

The anterior of the eye is the portion that we can see without any special instruments. It consists of the sclera (the white area) the conjunctiva (the clear tissue that covers the front of the eye), the cornea (the clear dome in the center where light enters the eye).

The cornea covers the iris (colored part of your eye) and the pupil (the black circle in the center). The pupil and iris work together to regulate the amount of light that enters the eye.
